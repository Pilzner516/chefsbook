ChefsBook Strategic Plan

The intelligent cooking platform that actually helps people cook

ChefsBook is positioned to become the dominant global cooking platform by doing what no competitor does: actually helping people cook. While competitors focus on recipe browsing (NYT Cooking), meal kit delivery (HelloFresh), or social sharing (Cookpad), ChefsBook combines all three with a unique technical advantage: an AI-powered cooking scheduler that coordinates multi-course dinner parties with timing precision no human could achieve.

The recipe app market is in flux. Yummly (Whirlpool) and AllRecipes (Dotdash Meredith) discontinued their mobile apps in 2024-2025, leaving a gap for a modern, AI-native platform.

Social Platform Strategy

ChefsBook has social infrastructure (follows, likes, comments, DMs) but lacks the identity layer that makes Strava sticky. Strava users open the app 35+ times/month because it validates their athletic identity. ChefsBook must validate cooking identity.

The Strava Playbook for Cooking

Strava ChefsBook Equivalent
Kudos (one-tap validation) "Looks delicious!" reaction
Segments (competitive routes) Recipe ratings / leaderboards
Clubs (group identity) Cuisine communities (Thai food lovers)
Year in Review "Your Year in Cooking"
Activity feed Discover feed with "Cooked it!" posts

Key Social Features

C

"Cooked it!" Posts

Social proof that a recipe was actually made. User finishes Cook Mode, shares a photo, original creator gets notified, recipe shows "X people cooked this".

Very High Impact

Cooking Streaks

Duolingo-style daily consistency tracking. Milestones at 7, 30, 100, 365 days. Push notifications to prevent streak breaks.

Stack Overflow-style karma. Points for recipes cooked (+10), saves (+2), likes (+1), challenges (+50), streaks (+100). Profile badges and leaderboards.

Dinner party planning with friends. Host creates menu, guests claim courses, dietary restrictions aggregated, combined shopping list.
