Welcome

Your kitchen has never been this organised.

ChefsBook is a recipe manager built for how real cooks actually work — saving from websites, snapping pages from old cookbooks, speaking ideas aloud, and planning the week while standing at the fridge.

Whether you collect recipes obsessively, cook from scratch every night, or just want to stop losing that one great pasta recipe you found three years ago — ChefsBook brings it all together. It runs on your phone and in your browser, stays in sync, and works just as well offline as on.

This guide covers everything: how to get recipes in, how to organise and discover them, how to plan your week, and how to share what you love. It's written for new users getting started, experienced users unlocking features they haven't tried yet, and anyone curious about what makes ChefsBook different.

A note on AI

ChefsBook uses AI throughout — not to replace your cooking instincts, but to handle the tedious parts. Extracting recipes from chaotic web pages, suggesting how to group your shopping, translating ingredients into your language, generating a whole week of meals in seconds. The AI is always working quietly in the background so you can focus on the cooking.

New Here?

Up and running in 5 minutes.

This is the fastest path from a fresh account to a working recipe collection. Do these five things first — everything else in this guide builds on them.

1

Create your account at chefsbk.app

Go to chefsbk.app and tap Create Account. Enter your email and choose a password. Your account is confirmed instantly — no waiting for a verification email. On the next screen, pick a @username: this becomes your permanent profile URL (chefsbk.app/u/yourusername), so choose something you're happy with. You can change your display name later, but not your username.

2

Upgrade to Chef (recipe creation requires a paid plan)

The free tier lets you browse the community and manage a shopping list, but creating and importing your own recipes requires the Chef plan ($4.99/month). Go to Settings → Your Plan → See Plans and choose Chef. If you want to try before committing, the community recipe feed is worth exploring first — you can plan meals and build a shopping list from public recipes on the free tier.

3

Import your first recipe

Find a recipe you love online, copy the URL, and paste it into the Import & Scan page. ChefsBook will extract the title, ingredients, steps, and a cover image automatically. Review the result, make any edits, and tap Save. That's it — your first recipe is in your collection. Most imports take under 10 seconds.

4

Set your language and units

Open the sidebar and look for the language selector (web) or go to Settings (mobile). Pick your preferred language — the entire app switches instantly, including all navigation and labels. While you're there, set your preferred units (metric or imperial) — all recipe quantities will convert automatically from now on. Both settings sync across your phone and browser.

5

Create a shopping list and add your recipe

Go to Shopping in the sidebar, tap New List, choose a store, and give the list a name. Then open the recipe you just imported, tap Add to Shopping List, and choose that list. ChefsBook will scale the ingredients to your serving size, assign each item to a supermarket department, and suggest what to actually buy. Your first AI-organised shopping list is ready.

What to explore next

Once those five steps are done: try scanning a printed recipe from a cookbook using your phone camera (Chapter 1), set up a meal plan for the week (Chapter 5), or install the Chrome extension to import recipes from any website with one click (Chapter 8). The onboarding tips in the app will also guide you through the main features as you navigate — look for the small tooltip bubbles on your first few visits to each screen.

📱 Download the Android app

The mobile app has features the web doesn't — multi-page scanning, dish identification from photos, Instagram import, and offline shopping lists. Install it and sign into the same account. Everything syncs.

🧩 Install the Chrome extension

Browse any recipe website as normal. When you find something you want, click the ChefsBook button in your toolbar. The recipe imports in the background while you keep browsing. Find the extension at chefsbk.app/extension.

Chapter One

Importing Recipes

Eight different ways to get a recipe into ChefsBook — from a quick URL paste to photographing a book you've owned for twenty years.

🔗

Paste a URL AI-Assisted

Copy any recipe link — from NYT Cooking, Serious Eats, Smitten Kitchen, or a random food blog — and paste it into ChefsBook. Our import engine reads the page's structured data first, then fills in any missing pieces using AI. Ingredients, steps, cooking time, servings, cuisine, and even a cover photo are all extracted automatically. If a site has a particularly tricky format or blocks scrapers, ChefsBook falls back to smarter extraction methods. You'll rarely need to edit anything by hand.

📷

Scan a Recipe AI Vision

Point your phone camera at any printed recipe — from a cookbook, a magazine page, a handwritten card, or a printout. ChefsBook's AI reads the image and converts it into a fully structured recipe with ingredients and numbered steps. Long recipes? Use multi-page scanning: tap "Add page" to scan up to five pages in one go, and ChefsBook stitches them into a single complete recipe. The first scanned page is automatically saved as a cover photo so you always know where the recipe came from.

🎙️

Speak a Recipe AI

Have a recipe in your head? Just say it out loud. Open the Speak a Recipe screen, tap Record, and describe your recipe however comes naturally — "so you start with about 500 grams of flour, then add two eggs and some olive oil..." ChefsBook listens, transcribes, and uses AI to turn your spoken description into a properly formatted recipe with structured ingredients and steps. Add a cover photo before saving — either from your camera roll or let ChefsBook find a beautiful image automatically using its Pexels integration.

🍜

Import from Instagram Mobile

Found a great recipe on Instagram? Share the post directly to ChefsBook from the Instagram app (tap the share button and choose "ChefsBook") or paste the URL in the import screen. ChefsBook extracts the post image and caption, then uses AI to identify the recipe and structure it properly. If the post shows a finished dish but no recipe text, ChefsBook identifies the dish and drops you into a flow to generate a recipe from scratch — right from the photo.

▶

Import from YouTube AI

Paste a YouTube cooking video URL and ChefsBook fetches the video's transcript and metadata, then uses AI to extract the recipe with timestamps intact. If the video description contains a written recipe, ChefsBook uses that first. The result is a clean, usable recipe with step timings you can actually follow while cooking — no more pausing and rewinding.

📄

Upload a File

Have a recipe collection as a file? ChefsBook accepts PDF, Word (.docx), CSV, JSON, and plain text files. Upload from the Import & Scan tab and ChefsBook extracts all recipes in one pass. This is the fastest way to migrate from another app or digitise a document archive.

🔖

Batch Bookmark Import Web

Export your browser bookmarks from Chrome, Safari, or Firefox as an HTML file and upload it to ChefsBook. It reads your entire bookmark folder structure, maps each folder to a recipe category, and imports every URL as a recipe — working through them in the background so you can keep using the app while it runs. Hundreds of saved recipe links, imported in one go.

📸

Identify a Dish from a Photo AI Vision

See a dish on a menu, at a restaurant, or in a photo and want a recipe for it? Point your camera at it. ChefsBook analyses the image, identifies what the dish likely is, asks a couple of quick clarifying questions (cuisine? any details you remember?), and then generates a complete recipe for it. You can also search for similar recipes in the ChefsBook community first, or go straight to AI generation. The scanned photo becomes your recipe's cover image.

📚

Import from a Physical Cookbook AI Vision

Photograph the cover or spine of a cookbook, or type its ISBN, and ChefsBook identifies the book automatically using Google Books and OpenLibrary. It reads the cover photo to extract the title and author, then lets you search and select specific recipes to import. ChefsBook searches the web for each recipe, extracts the text, and saves it with a "from Book Title" tag and attribution. Your physical cookbook collection becomes fully searchable.

After any import, ChefsBook prompts you to choose a cover photo — it will automatically suggest three matching images from Pexels. You can also use the imported website's original image, a photo you scanned, or snap something yourself.

"ChefsBook is the first app where I've actually managed to import my entire recipe collection — from bookmarks, cookbooks, and handwritten cards — in a single afternoon."

Chapter Two

AI-Powered Features

ChefsBook uses AI throughout — but always to save you time, never to get in the way. Here's what it can do for you.

🏷️ Auto-Tagging

When you import or save a recipe, ChefsBook automatically suggests 5–8 relevant tags based on the recipe's title, ingredients, and cuisine. Tags like "quick", "vegetarian", "weeknight", "italian" appear as suggestions — tap to add the ones that fit. You can also tap "Auto-tag my recipes" in the search screen to tag your entire collection in one go.

🌍 Real-Time Translation

Every recipe can be translated into your preferred language. ChefsBook first translates just the title (fast and cheap) so you can browse your collection in your language, then does a full translation of ingredients and steps on demand when you open the recipe. Translations are cached so the recipe is always ready instantly the second time.

🛒 Smart Shopping Units

When you add a recipe to your shopping list, ChefsBook's AI suggests the right purchase unit for each ingredient. "125g parmesan" becomes "1 block (200g)" — the actual thing you'd buy at the supermarket, not just the recipe quantity. This means less mental arithmetic at the store.

📅 AI Meal Planner

Tell ChefsBook your dietary preferences, cuisine style, effort level, and how many days you want to plan — and the AI builds a complete weekly meal plan using recipes from your own collection plus public community recipes. Review, swap, or remove individual meals before saving the plan.

🍳 Recipe Generation

When you photograph a dish, describe one out loud, or import a photo without a matching recipe, ChefsBook can generate a complete recipe from scratch. It takes into account any clarifying details you provide — cuisine, dietary needs, serving size — and produces a properly structured recipe you can edit and save.

🔍 Smart Import Completion

When a web page import is missing sections — no serving size listed, or instructions only as a paragraph — ChefsBook's AI fills in the gaps automatically and marks those sections as "AI-assisted" so you always know what was extracted vs. what was suggested. Nothing is changed silently.

✦

AI Content Moderation

ChefsBook automatically reviews every new recipe for appropriate content as it comes in. This runs invisibly for the vast majority of recipes — you'll never notice it. It protects the community from spam and ensures the public recipe feed stays useful and on-topic. If a recipe is flagged, you'll be notified with a clear explanation.

Cost-transparent AI

ChefsBook uses different AI models for different tasks — faster, lighter models for classification work (tagging, moderation, purchase units) and more powerful models for complex jobs (full recipe extraction, generation, translation). This keeps the app fast and affordable, and means AI features are available on every plan, not locked behind expensive tiers.

Chapter Three

Organising Your Collection

A great recipe manager isn't just a place to store recipes — it's a place you can actually find them when you need them.

🏷️

Tags

Tags are your most flexible organisational tool. Add them manually, let AI suggest them, or both. Tags like "quick", "family favourite", "christmas", "date night", "toddler-friendly" can cut across cuisines and courses in a way that rigid folders never can. Use the search screen to filter by multiple tags at once — ChefsBook shows only recipes that match every tag you've selected.

🍕

Cuisines & Courses

Every recipe has a cuisine (Italian, Japanese, Mexican…) and a course (starter, main, dessert, breakfast…). ChefsBook has 31 cuisines built in with a searchable dropdown — type to filter, or scroll and pick. These fields make the search and filter system genuinely useful: when you want something Italian that's quick enough for a weeknight, two filter taps get you there.

🔀

Recipe Versions

Made your grandmother's lasagne but changed the béchamel? Save it as a new version rather than overwriting the original. Versions are linked to their parent recipe — a pill on the recipe card shows "3 versions" and a tap lets you pick which one to open. Great for recipes you've iterated on, or for keeping a "classic" and a "weeknight shortcut" side by side. Each version can have its own label and description.

👁️

Visibility: Private, Shared Link, Public

Every recipe has a privacy level. Private means only you can see it. Shared link means anyone with the link can view it — great for sending a recipe to a friend without making it publicly searchable. Public means it appears in the community feed and can be found by other ChefsBook users. You can change visibility at any time.

❤️

Favourites & Pins

Tap the heart on any recipe card to add it to your Favourites. Tap the pin icon to pin a recipe so it always appears at the top of your collection. Both are visible in the filter pills on the dashboard — one tap to see only your favourites or only your pinned recipes.

🖼️

Recipe Photos

Add your own photos to any recipe — 1 per recipe on Chef and Family plans, up to 5 on Pro. Photos appear as a swipeable gallery at the top of the recipe, and the one you set as "primary" appears on recipe cards throughout the app. Long-press any thumbnail to set it as the cover. Your own kitchen photos make the whole collection feel genuinely yours.

📓

Cooking Notes

Every recipe has its own cooking journal. Add a note after you cook it — what you changed, how it went, what you'd do differently next time. Notes are private, timestamped, and searchable. Over time they build into a genuine record of your history with a recipe, far more useful than a star rating.

Use the grid, list, and table view modes on the dashboard to switch between a visual recipe gallery, a compact list for quick scanning, and a spreadsheet-style table when you want to sort by cuisine, date added, or recipe source.

Chapter Four

Search & Discovery

Search your own collection, explore the community, follow cooks whose recipes you love, and discover what people are cooking right now.

Searching Your Collection

The search bar on the dashboard searches across recipe titles, descriptions, cuisine, ingredients, tags, and notes all at once. As you type, results update instantly. Use the filter pills below the search bar to narrow down by cuisine, course, dietary requirement, or tag — and combine as many filters as you need. You can also sort by date added, alphabetical order, or most recently edited.

Full-Text Search

Type "chicken thighs" and ChefsBook finds every recipe that mentions chicken thighs — in the title, in the ingredients, anywhere. The search is fast and understands partial matches, so "choc" finds "chocolate".

Filter Stack

Combine filters freely — "Italian + quick + vegetarian" shows only recipes matching all three. Active filters appear as dismissable pills so it's always clear what's applied. One tap removes any filter.

People Search

Search by username to find other ChefsBook cooks. Results show their avatar, @username, and follower count. Tap to visit their profile and browse their public recipes.

All Recipes Toggle

Switch between "My Recipes" and "All Recipes" to search the entire public ChefsBook community, not just your own collection. Discover recipes uploaded by other cooks and save the ones you want.

✦

Following Chefs & the What's New Feed

Visit any cook's profile and tap Follow to subscribe to their public recipes. Once you're following a few people, the What's New feed shows you a live stream of their latest uploads — recipe card, @username, and how long ago it was posted. It's the best way to find recipes from cooks whose taste you trust, rather than trawling algorithm-driven food websites.

The community is growing

ChefsBook's public recipe feed is shared across all users. Every public recipe you add becomes part of a searchable community library. When someone else saves one of your recipes, you'll see it in your notifications.

Chapter Five

Meal Planning

Plan your week in minutes, not hours. ChefsBook's meal planner handles everything from assigning a recipe to Tuesday dinner to generating the whole week automatically with AI.

The Weekly Calendar

The Meal Plan screen shows a calendar for the current week — Monday through Sunday — with slots for Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snack each day. Tap any slot to add a recipe. A search panel slides in so you can pick from your own recipes or search the entire community. Each meal card on the calendar shows the recipe image, title, daypart (colour-coded), and serving count — tap either pill to change it on the spot.

1

Add a recipe to a day

Tap any empty slot on the calendar. Search by title, cuisine, or tag. Tap a recipe to add it, choose Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snack, and set the serving count.

2

Adjust on the card

Each meal card shows a daypart pill (bottom left) and servings pill (bottom right). Tap either to edit without opening the recipe — great for adjusting when your plans change at the last minute.

3

Send a day or week to your shopping list

Tap the cart icon on any day card, or use "Add Week to List" to send all ingredients to a shopping list. ChefsBook scales quantities to your serving counts and merges duplicates intelligently. More on this in Chapter 6.

✦

AI Meal Plan Wizard

Don't want to pick every recipe manually? The AI Meal Planner generates a complete week's meals in seconds. Walk through the 4-step wizard:

Step 1 — Days & Meals

Choose which days you need meals for and which meal slots (breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack) to fill. Skip the ones you handle yourself.

Step 2 — Preferences

Set dietary requirements, preferred cuisines, and effort level. "Weeknight quick" gives you under-30-minute meals; "weekend project" gives you something worth spending an afternoon on.

Step 3 — Sources

Choose whether to pull from your own recipes only, include community recipes, or both. The AI prioritises recipes you've already saved and cooked.

After the wizard runs, review each slot individually — swap out anything that doesn't feel right, or remove meals from days you've changed your mind about. When you're happy, save the plan and all meals land on your calendar.

ChefsBook warns you if you're adding a recipe with a very different serving size from other meals on the same day — a gentle heads-up so you don't end up making 10 portions of pasta alongside 2 portions of salad.

Chapter Six

Shopping Lists

Shopping lists in ChefsBook aren't just lists — they're organised by store, sorted by supermarket department, and smart enough to merge duplicates so you never buy double.

Creating a List

Tap "New Shopping List" and choose a store — either pick from ones you've saved before (Whole Foods, ShopRite, your local deli) or add a new one. ChefsBook fetches the store's logo automatically and uses it throughout the app for visual recognition. Give the list a name (or use the auto-filled suggestion) and you're ready.

✦

How Items Are Organised

Items are grouped into 13 supermarket departments — Produce, Meat & Seafood, Dairy & Eggs, Baking, Pasta & Grains, Canned & Jarred, and so on. When you add a recipe, ChefsBook's AI assigns each ingredient to its most likely department automatically. When you're at the store, you can work through the list aisle by aisle rather than jumping back and forth.

📦 Purchase Units

The purchase unit (shown in red) tells you what to actually buy — "1 can (400g)" rather than "400g". The usage amount (shown in green) shows how much the recipe needs. Both are visible on each item so you always know whether you have enough.

🔀 View Modes

Switch between three views: Department (aisle-by-aisle), Recipe (grouped by which recipe needs each item), and A–Z (alphabetical for quick scanning). The font size is adjustable — three settings, persisted between sessions.

📋 Consolidated Lists

If you have multiple shopping lists at the same store, a special All [Store] entry appears at the top — tap it to see every item from every list combined, with duplicates merged and quantities totalled. One view, one store, one trip.

📶 Offline Mode

Your shopping lists are cached on your device. If you lose signal in the supermarket (it happens), you can still check off items, edit quantities, and add new ones. Everything syncs back when you're connected again — nothing is lost.

✦

Adding Items from Recipes

From any recipe detail page, tap Add to Shopping List. Choose which list to add to, confirm the serving size (ChefsBook adjusts all quantities to match), and the items appear in the list grouped by department. If an ingredient is already on the list, quantities are merged rather than duplicated. You'll see a "X new, Y merged" confirmation.

You can add an entire week's meal plan to a shopping list in one tap. ChefsBook reads the serving counts from each meal card, scales all ingredients accordingly, merges duplicates across recipes, and sends everything to your list. A full week of groceries, sorted by department, in under five seconds.

Chapter Seven

Social & Sharing

Share a single recipe with a link, build a following, comment and discuss, or send a direct message to someone whose cooking you admire.

🖨️

Print a Recipe

Tap the print button on any recipe and choose your options — include the recipe image, include comments, or strip everything back to ingredients and steps only. The page prints cleanly with no browser chrome, ads, or navigation bars. Great for sticking on the fridge or handing to someone who doesn't use the app.

📄

Download as PDF Pro

Export any recipe as a beautifully formatted PDF — clean layout with a red header line, cover photo, ingredients list, numbered steps, and a branded footer on every page. Before generating, choose whether to include the recipe image and any comments. The file is named automatically ("ChefsBook — Thai Chicken Satay.pdf") and is ready to email, save, or archive permanently.

🔗

Share by Link

Any recipe can be shared as a link — tap the share button on a recipe and choose "Share via link". The URL (chefsbk.app/recipe/[id]) works in any browser. If the recipient isn't on ChefsBook, they see the recipe on a clean webpage with an option to save it to their own account or download the app. If they are on ChefsBook, the link opens directly in the app. Your @username is embedded in the link for attribution — if someone saves the recipe, you're credited automatically.

❤️

Likes

Tap the heart on any recipe to like it. The like count is visible on the recipe card. The recipe owner gets a notification when someone likes their recipe, including your @username. You can tap the like count on your own recipe to see a list of everyone who's liked it. Liking requires a Chef plan or above.

💬

Comments

Tap "Add a comment" below any recipe to leave a note, ask a question, or share a variation you tried. Comments are threaded — reply directly to any comment at any depth. Tap "N more replies" to expand nested threads. The recipe owner and parent commenter are notified of new comments and replies. Comments appear on all recipe pages regardless of your plan. Recipe owners can moderate comments, and all comments are reviewed for appropriate content automatically.

✉️

Direct Messages

Visit any cook's profile and tap Message to start a private conversation. Messages support multi-line text with proper formatting. The conversations panel shows all your threads with unread badges. New messages trigger a notification on the bell icon in the dashboard header. All messages are moderated for appropriate content automatically.

📱

Social Sharing Pro

Generate a ready-to-post caption for Instagram, Pinterest, or Facebook — ChefsBook writes platform-optimised text with hashtags based on your recipe. Copy it, download the recipe image, or open directly in the relevant app. Takes about five seconds to go from recipe to ready-to-post.

👥

Following

Follow any public ChefsBook user to subscribe to their recipe uploads. Your profile shows your follower and following counts. Other users can follow you. The What's New feed on the home screen shows a chronological stream of recipes from the people you follow — no algorithms, no promoted content, just the people whose cooking you trust.

Recipe Attribution

ChefsBook tracks where every recipe comes from. When you import a recipe from a website, the source URL is saved and displayed as a small pill below the recipe title. When someone shares a recipe with you and you save it, your profile is shown as "shared by" alongside the original creator's credit. Attribution is permanent and visible — original creators are always acknowledged.

Chapter Eight

Cookbooks & Techniques

Your physical cookbook shelf, finally searchable. And a growing library of cooking techniques that explain the how behind the what.

Cookbook Intelligence

ChefsBook can read your physical cookbook collection and make it searchable. There are three ways to identify a book:

1

Photograph the cover

AI reads the cover photo and identifies the book from the title and author it can see. Works for most cookbooks with a readable cover.

2

Scan the barcode (ISBN)

Point your camera at the barcode on the back. ChefsBook looks up the ISBN in Google Books and OpenLibrary, retrieves the full book details and cover image automatically.

3

Type the title

Search by title or author if the other methods don't work. ChefsBook searches the book databases and lets you pick the correct edition.

Once identified, ChefsBook generates a table of contents for the book using AI and presents the recipes as browsable cards with an "Import" button next to each one. Select a recipe, and ChefsBook searches the web for that specific recipe, extracts the text, and saves it to your collection — tagged with the cookbook title, attributed to the original author, and ready to add to your meal plans and shopping lists.

✦

Techniques

Alongside recipes, ChefsBook maintains a library of cooking techniques — the skills and methods that make the difference between a recipe working and not working. Techniques have their own dedicated section in the sidebar and a different visual style: a two-column layout showing tips, common mistakes, and step-by-step instructions side by side.

You can add techniques manually, import them from URLs (ChefsBook automatically detects whether a URL is a recipe or a technique), or let the Chrome browser extension capture them as you browse. Techniques are searchable, taggable, and linkable from recipes — when you're reading a recipe that calls for "blind baking", you can link through to the technique that explains it.

The ChefsBook browser extension (available for Chrome) adds an import button to every recipe and technique page you visit. One click, and it's in your collection — no copying, no switching apps. Install it from the Extension page in the sidebar.

Metric & Imperial

Set your preferred measurement system in the sidebar (web) or Settings (mobile). The conversion happens in real time and applies everywhere: recipe cards, recipe detail, the shopping list, and the meal planner.

🎓 Onboarding Help Tips

First-time users see a set of guided tooltips that point out key features as you navigate the app. Re-enable them anytime from Settings → Help Tips if you want a refresher. You can also dismiss individual tips without turning the whole system off.

🔔 Notifications

The bell icon in the dashboard header shows unread notifications across five categories: All, Comments, Likes, Followers, and Moderation. Mark all as read in one tap, or click individual notifications to go directly to the relevant recipe or profile.

ChefsBook accepts up to five pages per session and passes all the images to the AI in one go, which stitches them into a single complete recipe. The first page you scan is saved automatically as the recipe's cover photo. This is the fastest way to digitise a physical cookbook collection.

🍜

Dish Identification from a Photo

Saw something at a restaurant you want to recreate? Open the Scan tab and photograph the dish. ChefsBook's AI analyses the image to identify what it is, then asks a few quick questions — what cuisine do you think it is? any specific details you can see? It offers a list of matching dish names to pick from, or lets you confirm its best guess. From there you can search the ChefsBook community for existing recipes for that dish, or ask the AI to generate a recipe from scratch using the photo as reference. The scanned photo becomes your recipe's cover image automatically.

📱

Instagram Import (Share Sheet)

While browsing Instagram, find a recipe post and tap the share button in the Instagram app. ChefsBook appears in your share sheet — tap it, and the import runs in the background. ChefsBook fetches the post's image and caption, identifies the recipe, and saves it to your collection. If the post is a photo of a finished dish with no written recipe, ChefsBook identifies the dish and takes you through the generation flow using the Instagram image as reference. Private posts and those that fail to fetch show a clear error with a manual entry fallback.

🎙️

Speak a Recipe

Tap the microphone on the Scan tab and describe your recipe out loud — as naturally as you'd tell a friend. "So you start by browning the butter until it smells nutty, then add about 200 grams of brown sugar..." ChefsBook transcribes your voice in real time using your phone's speech recognition, then sends the full transcript to the AI, which structures it into a properly formatted recipe with a title, ingredient list (with quantities), and numbered steps. Before saving, you're prompted to choose a cover photo from your camera roll or let ChefsBook find a Pexels image automatically.

🔔

Real-time Sync via Supabase Realtime

The mobile app uses WebSocket connections to your ChefsBook backend for live updates. When someone replies to your comment, likes your recipe, or sends you a message while you have the app open, the notification badge updates instantly — no pull-to-refresh needed. Shopping list items you check off on one device disappear from the list on the other in seconds.

📶

Offline Shopping Lists

Your shopping lists are cached locally using the device's file system storage. When you walk into a supermarket and lose signal (it happens in basements and large stores), your lists are fully accessible — you can check items off, adjust quantities, and add new items manually. ChefsBook tracks all these as "pending edits" and syncs them back to the server as soon as your connection returns. The list shows a subtle amber offline banner so you always know when you're working from the local cache.

✦

What's the same on web and mobile

The core experience is fully consistent across both platforms. Everything in the following list works identically on the web app and the Android app:

📚 Recipe library

Full CRUD on your recipes, inline editing, tags, cuisines, versions, visibility controls, photo gallery, cooking notes, and attribution pills.

Create lists, assign to stores, add from recipes (with AI purchase units), three view modes (dept / recipe / A–Z), font size controls, and consolidated multi-list views per store.

📖 Cookbooks & techniques

Create and browse cookbooks, import from physical books, and access the techniques library with tips, mistakes, and step-by-step instructions.

The Android app is a native app built with Expo SDK 54 and React Native — not a web view. It uses the platform's own navigation, gestures, and keyboard behaviour, which means it feels like a proper phone app rather than a wrapped website.

Photograph a cookbook page and it's searchable in seconds. Describe a recipe out loud and it's saved and tagged before you reach the kitchen counter.
