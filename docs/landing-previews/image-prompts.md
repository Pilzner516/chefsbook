## Style Reference Notes

**Adjusting prompts per concept:**

- **For Concept A (Unified Kitchen)** — lean the prompt toward *white marble, light oak, linen, Scandinavian aesthetic, bright diffused window light, neutral-to-warm white color temperature*. Keep compositions spacious and organized. Any color accent should be a single pomodoro-red element (tomato, bell pepper, chili).

- **For Concept B (Operating System)** — lean the prompt toward *dark slate, black marble, cast iron, aged copper, single-source dramatic side lighting, deep shadows, cinematic chiaroscuro*. Color temperature cool with warm garnet and copper highlights. Compositions should feel intentional and restrained — fewer elements, more negative space, more drama.

- **For Concept C (Beautiful Food)** — lean the prompt toward *butcher block, terracotta, warm wood, linen the color of wheat, golden-hour sunlight streaming from a low angle*. Very warm color temperature, joyful and abundant composition. More elements, more human warmth, more life.

**Universal reminders to keep in every prompt:**
- Editorial food photography, natural window light, shallow depth of field, 50mm lens, professional food styling
- No people's faces — hands only
- Specify surface and color temperature
- End with: high resolution, no text, no watermarks

**Aspect ratio guide:**
- Hero images: 1920x1080 (full-bleed or offset)
- Showcase/card images: 1200x800 (balanced hero/text layout)
- Portrait-oriented alternate: 800x1200 if vertical composition is preferred for mobile hero variants

**When iterating with Nano Banana:**
- Generate 4 variants per prompt, pick the strongest, then inpaint/refine.
- If a generated dish looks unrealistic or over-AI'd (too glossy, plastic highlights, warped cutlery), add "natural, imperfect, real-food textures, slight asymmetry, visible natural variation" to the prompt.
- Avoid prompts that require legible text on screens or labels — AI image models produce garbled text; keep phone screens blurred or blank and add the real UI later.
